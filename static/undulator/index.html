Aorangi Undulator
Deep in the Aorangi Forest

The Aorangi Undulator is a point-to-point trail race through the heart of the Aorangi Forest Park — one of New Zealand's most remote and seldom-visited wilderness areas. Expect relentless ridge undulations, dense native bush, and long sections where the only sounds are your own breathing and the wind in the tōtara.

A demanding and beautiful course that rewards runners who respect the terrain. A WRECKd event, proudly put on by the Wilderness Running and Endurance Collective Inc.

The Course | ~38km / 1,450m △
Ridge Starting at Putangirua Pinnacles, the route immediately climbs onto the main Aorangi Ridge. The views open fast — coast to the east, Wairarapa to the north. A long undulating traverse rewards those who find their rhythm early.
Forest Dropping into the forest interior, this is the wild heart of the course. Dense podocarp canopy, rooted tracks, and creek crossings that demand full attention. Navigation is essential — markers are sparse by design.
Saddle The climb to Aorangi Peak is the high point in every sense. At 983m, the summit saddle offers panoramic views. The descent is technical and steep — loose shingle and rooted gullies leading to the valley floor.
River The final leg follows the Mangatoetoe Stream through broad flats and river bends, easing you toward the finish at Kaitoke. Legs tired, spirits restored by the sound of moving water.
